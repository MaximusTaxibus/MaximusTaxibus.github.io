About me

My name is Magdalena Schreiber and I am head of the international office at Hochschule Esslingen by day and learning to program by night. My background is in the humanities and social sciences: I studied Literature, Art, Media, English, History and Cultural Studies at the Universities of Constance and Tübingen in Germany, Durham University in the UK and Bogazici University in Turkey. I have always been fascinated with culture (singular) and cultures (plural) and therefore decided to not only study it and spend time in different countries and contexts, but also start my career in international cooperation and intercultural education. At my current job I lead a team of ten. We are responsible for international student exchanges, cooperation with international partners as well as the larger internationalisation process and strategy of the university. I have always been interested in communications, media and new technology. In 2017 I discovered my interest in programming through an online course and was amazed by the amount of high-quality resources being available on the internet. Since then I have been hooked on the idea of learning to program and started with basic web development with HTML and CSS. I recently rediscovered my fascination and I am currently taking a Web development boot camp class
